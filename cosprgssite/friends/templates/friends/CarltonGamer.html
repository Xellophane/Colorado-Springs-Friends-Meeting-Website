Biographical Information

Carlton is a composer, music theorist, sometime pianist, and teacher.

Carlon's father, formative years were spent in Germany, his mother's in Vienna and Paris. Carlton's father, Carl, was a Methodist minister and professor of political science, while Carlton's mother, Alice, a sculptor. Both were fluent in German and French. Through Carlton and his brother Robert were raised in the Midwest, their family life was cosmopolitan. From the age of seven, Carlton studied piano and composition with Tanya and Hubert Kessler --she a pianist, he a composer and theorist at the University of Illinois-- who were themselves Viennese. From them and from his aunt, Helena Gamer, who taught German at the University of Chicago and who had an extensive library of musical scores and took Carlton to chamber music concerts and to the opera, he acquired a love and knowledge of European classical music. In college, Carlton discovered the works of Stravinsky, Bartok, Hindemith, and Berg. The music of Roger Sessions, a distinguished composer with whoem Carlton had studied in the 1950's, was rooted in the same European tradition. Carlton's own music, then, bears the stamp of that heritage.

Carlton has had a strong and sustained interests in the history of music; political and cultural history; art and art history; Modernism; philosophy and aestetics; mathematics (especially discrete mathematics); Christian mysticism; Vedanta and Buddhism (especially Rinzai Zen); poetry; English and American literature; various European languages and literatures (Latin, German, French, Spanish, Italian, and Russian); Japanese language, literature and culture; Asian literature; literary theory; women's studies; non-Western musics (espcially those of North India, Indonesia, and West Africa); opera; dance; and jazz. He is a Quaker and has been a long-time peace activist. Thos interests and involvements have informed his work.
